Buddybug UI Concepts

Calmer navigation. Cleaner reading. A more premium family feel.

These concept mockups are separate from the live app and are meant to show the strongest visual directions for Buddybug: a thumb-friendly signed-in shell, a less cluttered library, and a more elegant reader with quieter chrome.

Bottom navigation instead of menu-first routing Lighter browsing cards with one featured hero card Cleaner reader controls and slimmer scroll-away top bar
What This Is

A visual concept board only. It does not replace or alter the working app. It is designed to help us agree a direction before changing anything real.

  • Concept 1: a more accommodating signed-in app shell
  • Concept 2: a simpler, calmer library flow
  • Concept 3: a more premium and unobtrusive reader
Concept 1

Signed-in shell that feels like a family app, not a menu tree

The current signed-in app relies too heavily on the hamburger menu. This concept keeps the top area small and warm, then makes the main destinations thumb-reachable with a bottom tab bar. That creates easier navigation on phones and tablets and makes the app feel more intentional.

Why this helps Navigation becomes easier to learn, easier to reach, and more consistent across pages. The menu can become secondary instead of doing all the work.
Persistent bottom navigation Home, Library, Continue, and Settings stay available all the time, which removes unnecessary menu opening.
Compact top status strip The child profile and account state stay visible without turning the whole header into a heavy card.
Lighter content rhythm Main tasks feel like clear destination cards rather than many unrelated stacked panels.

Concept 2

Library that starts with a story, not a stack of decisions

The library should feel calmer and faster. This concept keeps the route choice visible, but compresses it into a neat segmented control, keeps one featured recommendation, and then gets the user into the browsing grid straight away.

Why this helps The first screen becomes easier to understand. Parents can choose a route quickly, spot a featured story, and browse immediately without reading through multiple stacked cards.
Compact route control The current route buttons become one elegant segmented control with less vertical space.
One hero recommendation Featured content stays strong, but no longer competes visually with every other part of the page.
Lighter browse cards Regular story cards become easier to scan, while premium cues like audio badges stay visible.

Concept 3

Reader that gets out of the way and lets the story breathe

The reader is already strong. This concept pushes it further by shrinking the top chrome, keeping narration controls compact, and visually prioritising the illustrated page and story text over everything else.

Why this helps The story becomes the focal point. Parents still keep orientation and controls, but the reader feels more premium, less busy, and better suited to longer reading sessions.
Smaller floating top bar Back, title, and progress stay available, but the top card no longer dominates the reading space.
Compact narration controls Only the two essential buttons sit below the page, which keeps the reading flow calm and obvious.
Continuous page rhythm The next page starts to peek below, making vertical reading feel intentional and inviting.
